Gallo Pinto

Description

Gallo pinto is a traditional breakfast food in Costa Rica. Literally translated to mean "spotted rooster", this dish consists of rice and beans (the beans being the "spots") and spices. The most important of theses spices being the local seasoning sauce Salsa Lizano.

This dish can be found on the breakfast table in most homes in Costa Rica and definitely in almost all the typical restaurants called "sodas". This dish is often served alongside eggs, tortillas, fresh cheese, sweet fried plantains, natilla (the Costa Rican version of sour cream) and of course, fresh coffee. And if you're really lucky, sometimes bacon or sausage.

Ingredients

  • 2 tbps. neutral flavored oil; canola, vegetable, soy, etc
  • 1-2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 small onion, small diced
  • 1/2 sweet pepper, small diced
  • 2 cups cook white rice, day old preferred
  • 1 cup (1/2 can) cooked black or red beans
  • Some reserved liquid from the beans, about 2-3 large spoonfuls
  • Salsa Lizano*, to taste
  • Small handful fresh cilantro, rough chopped

Steps

  1. Add oil to a medium sized pan; heat over medium heat.
  2. Once pan is hot, add onions and sweet pepper to pan. Cook until they begin to soften.
  3. Add minced garlic; cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  4. Next, add rice and let warm in the oil for a few minutes.
  5. After rice has warmed, add beans to rice mixture, along with extra liquid.
  6. Add salsa Lizano.
  7. Let mixutre cook until all liquid has absorbed, about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  8. When the gallo pinto has finished cooking, gently fold in chopped cilantro.
  9. Serve.

Notes

    If you do not have access to Salsa Lizano, a steak sauce such as A1 has a similar flavor, but not exact.
